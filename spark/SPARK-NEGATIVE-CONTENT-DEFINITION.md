**SPARK NEGATIVE CONTENT DEFINITION – Clear Filter Rules**

**Purpose:**
To ensure SPARK only keeps signals that help the user win, while filtering out noise, toxicity, and irrelevant content.

---

### **What Counts as Negative Content (FILTER OUT)**

**1. Toxicity & Harm**
- Hate speech, slurs, discriminatory language
- Threats, violence, or aggressive language
- Bullying or personal attacks
- Extreme negativity that brings no value

**2. Pure Complaints (No Actionable Insight)**
- “This is shit”, “I hate this”, “Everything sucks” (no specific problem or solution mentioned)
- Endless venting with no direction
- Self-deprecating spirals that don’t lead to growth

**3. Irrelevant Filler**
- Random small talk unrelated to user’s mission
- Background noise conversations
- Unrelated YouTube/TikTok audio
- Off-topic tangents

**4. Low-Value Repetition**
- Repeating the same complaint multiple times without new insight
- Circular negative thinking with no progression

---

### **What Should Be KEPT (Even if Negative)**

**1. Constructive Criticism**
- “This campaign didn’t work because X. Next time I should try Y.”
- Specific feedback that leads to improvement

**2. Emotional Signals (with Context)**
- “I felt frustrated today because engagement was low” → Keep (energy + trigger)
- “I’m tired of doing the same thing every week” → Keep (motivation insight)

**3. Problem Statements (with Potential Solution)**
- “The council keeps removing my posters. I need a better way to stick them.”
- “My reach dropped after I posted about X. Maybe I should change my tone.”

**4. Honest Self-Reflection**
- “I’m struggling with consistency. I need a better system.”
- “I’m scared to post this because it might not land.”

---

### **Filter Decision Framework**

**SPARK asks these questions internally:**

1. Does this contain actionable insight or learning?
2. Can this help improve future campaigns, mindset, or strategy?
3. Is there a clear signal (idea, trend, emotion, location, creative spark)?
4. Is the negativity constructive or just venting?

**If YES to any** → Keep + assign weight
**If NO to all** → Filter out

---

### **Edge Cases**

- **Mixed content**: Keep the useful part, filter the rest
- **Emotional venting that leads to insight**: Keep the insight, note the emotion as context
- **User explicitly asks to keep something**: Always respect user override
- **User wants to hear full unfiltered version**: Available on request (with warning)

---

### **Transparency to User**

When SPARK filters:
- “I filtered out 12 minutes of venting and kept 4 signals that can actually help us win.”
- “I removed the negativity and kept the core idea: [insight]. Want to expand on it?”

User can always ask:
- “Show me what you filtered out” (with warning)
- “Play the full unfiltered version” (with confirmation)

---

**Goal:**
SPARK becomes a positive, mission-focused intelligence partner – not a therapist, not a complaint collector, but a strategic co-pilot that only keeps what helps the user build their empire.
