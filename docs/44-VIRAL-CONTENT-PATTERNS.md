**VIRAL CONTENT PATTERNS – Street Art & Guerrilla Marketing**

**Purpose:**
This document gives the agent clear, proven patterns that make street art and guerrilla drops go viral. Use these when helping users design campaigns.

---

### **Top 10 Viral Patterns (Ranked by Effectiveness)**

**1. Surprise & Unexpectedness (Highest Impact)**
- People share what shocks, delights, or confuses them.
- Best examples: Reverse graffiti that suddenly appears, oversized installations, optical illusions on streets.
- Agent Tip: Suggest drops in unexpected places (inside elevators, on the back of stop signs, projected on buildings at night).

**2. Strong Emotional Trigger**
- Laughter, anger, inspiration, sadness, or awe drive shares.
- Political or social commentary often performs well if executed cleanly.
- Personal stories (mental health, struggle, triumph) create deep connection.

**3. High Visual Impact**
- Bold colors, high contrast, clean composition.
- Photogenic drops get screenshot and reposted more.
- Agent Tip: Recommend simple, striking designs over complex ones for first drops.

**4. Relatability & Timeliness**
- Content that speaks to current events, local issues, or universal feelings spreads fast.
- Tapping into trending topics (elections, sports, music releases, memes) increases reach.

**5. Before/After or Process Documentation**
- Showing the creation process or the transformation (dirty wall → clean message) performs extremely well.
- People love seeing the "magic" happen.

**6. Interactive or Shareable Elements**
- QR codes, tear-off tabs, AR filters, or "find the next piece" games.
- Easy to photograph and repost = higher virality.

**7. Controversy (Use Carefully)**
- Edgy or slightly illegal drops can explode, but carry high risk.
- Best used when user has strong risk tolerance and legal backup.

**8. Mystery & Story**
- Campaigns that unfold over time (clues, chapters, reveals) keep people engaged and sharing.
- Lore-based drops work especially well with younger audiences.

**9. Local + Global Hook**
- Content that feels hyper-local but has universal appeal travels farthest.
- Example: A local issue presented in a way that resonates worldwide.

**10. High-Quality Documentation**
- Professional photos/videos of the drop itself are often more important than the drop.
- Agent Tip: Always include a documentation plan in every campaign.

---

### **Platform-Specific Patterns**

**Instagram:**
- Strong single-image impact + carousel storytelling performs best.
- Location tags + relevant hashtags still work.
- Reels of the creation process go further than static photos.

**TikTok:**
- Process videos, time-lapses, and "day in the life of a street artist" content dominate.
- Trending sounds + text overlays increase reach dramatically.
- Short, punchy videos (7-15 seconds) perform best.

**X (Twitter):**
- Controversial or funny takes on current events spread fastest.
- Thread format ("I did this drop and here's what happened") works very well.

---

### **Agent Usage Rules**

- When helping users design campaigns, always suggest at least 2-3 viral patterns from this list.
- Prioritize patterns that match the user’s risk tolerance and goals.
- For first-time users: Focus on Surprise + Visual Impact + Documentation (safest high-virality combo).
- For experienced users: Add Emotional Trigger + Story + Interactive elements.

**Final Truth:**
Viral success in street art comes from a mix of strong visual execution, emotional resonance, and smart documentation. The drop itself is only half the battle — how it’s captured and shared is equally important.
