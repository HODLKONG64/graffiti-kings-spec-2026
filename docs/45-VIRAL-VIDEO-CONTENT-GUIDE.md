**VIRAL VIDEO CONTENT GUIDE – Street Art & Guerrilla Drops**

**Purpose:**
This guide helps users (and the agent) create high-performing video content to document and spread their work. Covers both short-form (TikTok/Reels) and longer "mini Netflix doc" style.

---

### **1. SHORT-FORM VIDEO (7-60 seconds) – Highest Virality Potential**

**Best Performing Formats:**

**A. The Transformation (Most Viral)**
- Before → During → After in 15-30 seconds
- Example: Dirty wall → wheatpaste going up → final clean message
- Add text overlays: "3 minutes later..." or "From nothing to this"

**B. The Process (High Retention)**
- Time-lapse of creating the piece
- Quick cuts of hands, spray, brush, roller
- Text: "POV: You’re a street artist at 3am"

**C. The Reveal (Strong Hook)**
- Start with the final piece, then show how it was made
- Or start with a surprising location reveal

**D. The Reaction (Social Proof)**
- Film people walking by and reacting
- Or film yourself reacting to the finished drop

**Key Rules for Short-Form:**
- Hook in first 1-3 seconds
- Text overlays (most people watch without sound)
- Trending audio when possible
- Fast cuts, high energy
- End with a strong CTA ("Follow for more drops" or "Part 2 tomorrow")

---

### **2. MINI NETFLIX DOC STYLE (2-8 minutes) – Deeper Engagement**

**Best Structure:**

**Opening Hook (0-15 sec)**
- Start with the most dramatic moment (the drop going up, a powerful final shot, or a strong statement)

**The Story (30-90 sec)**
- Why you made this piece
- What it means to you
- The message behind it

**The Process (60-120 sec)**
- Show the full creation (sketching, prepping materials, going out at night, the actual drop)
- Be honest about the risk, the cold, the fear, the adrenaline

**The Impact (30-60 sec)**
- Reactions from people walking by
- How it felt seeing it the next day
- Any media coverage or messages you received

**The Reflection (30-60 sec)**
- What you learned
- What you would do differently
- Tease the next piece

**Key Rules for Longer Videos:**
- Strong narrative arc (beginning → middle → end)
- Good lighting and sound (use a cheap lav mic if possible)
- Music that matches the mood (not just trending audio)
- Chapters or text timestamps for longer videos
- End with a clear ask (subscribe, follow, share, support)

---

### **3. Content That Performs Best Right Now (2026)**

**Top Performing Themes:**
- "Day in the life of a street artist"
- "I turned my trauma into street art"
- "I dropped this in [controversial location] and this happened"
- "Before vs After" transformations
- "I only had £20 and made this campaign"
- "POV: You’re painting at 4am and the police drive by"

**Best Hooks (First 3 Seconds):**
- "This took me 4 hours and might get me arrested..."
- "I turned my worst day into this..."
- "They said I couldn’t do this legally... so I did it anyway"
- "Watch what happens when I drop this in the city centre at midnight"

---

### **4. Agent Advice Rules**

When helping users create video content:

- Always suggest both a short-form version AND a longer mini-doc version
- Recommend starting with the short version (easier to go viral)
- For the longer version, help them structure the story first
- Suggest filming the process even if they don’t plan to post it (you never know what will resonate)
- Remind them that documentation is often more important than the drop itself for building an audience

**Final Truth:**
The best video content doesn’t just show the art — it makes people feel something and want to share it. Focus on emotion, story, and surprise.
