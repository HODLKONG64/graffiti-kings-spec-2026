**VIRAL VIDEO CONTENT GUIDE – EXPANDED VERSION**

**Purpose:**
This guide helps users (and the agent) create high-performing video content to document and spread their street art and guerrilla drops. Covers both short-form (TikTok/Reels) and longer "mini Netflix doc" style. Expanded with more formats, hooks, and agent advice.

---

### **1. SHORT-FORM VIDEO (7-60 seconds) – Highest Virality Potential**

**Best Performing Formats:**

**A. The Transformation (Most Viral)**
- Before → During → After in 15-30 seconds
- Example: Dirty/blank wall → wheatpaste or stencil going up → final powerful message
- Add text overlays: "3 minutes later...", "From nothing to this", "Watch what happens"
- Strongest format for first-time viral potential

**B. The Process (High Retention)**
- Time-lapse of creating the piece (sketching, prepping, going out, the drop)
- Quick cuts of hands, spray can, brush, roller, wheatpaste application
- Text: "POV: You’re a street artist at 3am", "This took 4 hours"
- Very popular on TikTok and Instagram Reels

**C. The Reveal (Strong Hook)**
- Start with the final piece in all its glory, then show how it was made (or vice versa)
- Or start with a surprising location reveal ("I dropped this in the middle of [busy place]")
- Great for creating "wait, what?" moments

**D. The Reaction (Social Proof)**
- Film people walking by and reacting (surprise, confusion, laughter, photos being taken)
- Or film yourself reacting to the finished drop the next day
- Builds authenticity and shows real-world impact

**E. The POV / Day in the Life**
- "POV: You’re painting at 4am and the police drive by"
- "A day in the life of a street artist"
- Very high engagement on TikTok

**Key Rules for Short-Form:**
- Hook in first 1-3 seconds (most important rule)
- Text overlays (most people watch without sound)
- Trending audio when possible (but don’t force it if it doesn’t fit)
- Fast cuts, high energy, no dead space
- End with a strong CTA ("Follow for more drops", "Part 2 tomorrow", "Full story in bio")

### **2. MINI NETFLIX DOC STYLE (2-8 minutes) – Deeper Engagement & Loyalty**

**Best Structure:**

**Opening Hook (0-15 sec)**
- Start with the most dramatic moment (the drop going up, a powerful final shot, a strong statement, or a surprising reveal)
- Goal: Make people stop scrolling and watch

**The Story (30-90 sec)**
- Why you made this piece
- What it means to you personally
- The message or feeling behind it
- Be honest and vulnerable here — this is what creates connection

**The Process (60-120 sec)**
- Show the full creation journey (sketching, prepping materials, choosing the location, going out at night, the actual drop)
- Be honest about the risk, the cold, the fear, the adrenaline, the doubt
- This is where people feel like they’re with you

**The Impact (30-60 sec)**
- Reactions from people walking by (if filmed)
- How it felt seeing it the next day
- Any media coverage, messages, or conversations it started
- Real-world consequences (good or bad)

**The Reflection (30-60 sec)**
- What you learned from this drop
- What you would do differently next time
- How it changed you (even slightly)
- Tease the next piece or bigger vision

**Key Rules for Longer Videos:**
- Strong narrative arc (beginning → middle → end with emotional payoff)
- Good lighting and sound (use a cheap lav mic if possible — audio quality matters more than video quality)
- Music that matches the mood (not just trending audio)
- Chapters or text timestamps for longer videos (helps retention)
- End with a clear ask (subscribe, follow, share, support, join community)

### **3. Content That Performs Best Right Now (2026)**

**Top Performing Themes:**
- "Day in the life of a street artist" (process + reality)
- "I turned my trauma/mental health struggle into street art" (emotional + relatable)
- "I dropped this in [controversial or unexpected location] and this happened" (story + risk)
- Before vs After transformations (visual satisfaction)
- "I only had £20 and made this entire campaign" (resourcefulness + inspiration)
- "POV: You’re painting at 4am and the police drive by" (tension + reality)
- "I turned my worst day into this" (emotional + inspiring)

**Best Hooks (First 3 Seconds):**
- "This took me 4 hours and might get me arrested..."
- "I turned my worst day into this..."
- "They said I couldn’t do this legally... so I did it anyway"
- "Watch what happens when I drop this in the city centre at midnight"
- "I only had £20 and made this entire campaign"
- "POV: You’re a street artist at 3am"

### **4. Agent Advice Rules**

When helping users create video content:
- Always suggest both a short-form version AND a longer mini-doc version
- Recommend starting with the short version (easier to go viral, less pressure)
- For the longer version, help them structure the story first (this is where most people struggle)
- Suggest filming the process even if they don’t plan to post it (you never know what will resonate or become useful later)
- Remind them that documentation is often more important than the drop itself for building an audience
- Help them find the emotional hook in their story (this is what makes content shareable)

**Final Truth:**
The best video content doesn’t just show the art — it makes people feel something and want to share it. Focus on emotion, story, surprise, and authenticity. The drop is the beginning — the video is how it spreads.
